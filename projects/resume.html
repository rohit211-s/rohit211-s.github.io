Comprehensive Bank Loan Analysis Using SQL, PowerBI, and Tableau

  • Github URL: Project Link
  • Tools Used:

    Microsoft SQL Server, Power BI, Tableau, MS Excel

This project provides a comprehensive exploration of a bank’s loan portfolio, combining SQL-driven data extraction with powerful visualization tools to reveal critical trends and performance indicators. The analysis covers multiple perspectives—temporal, geographic, and categorical—delving into aspects like loan application volumes, repayment patterns, borrower demographics, and key financial metrics such as total funded amounts, average interest rates, and loan statuses. By segmenting loans into performing and non-performing categories and examining factors like DTI ratios and loan purposes, the project offers a thorough understanding of portfolio strengths and areas for strategic improvement.

Through a methodical approach, I leveraged Microsoft SQL Server for robust data management, ensuring reliable storage and retrieval of complex loan data. Excel was instrumental in preliminary data cleaning, validation, and early analysis, establishing a strong foundation for in-depth insights. I utilized Power BI and Tableau to bring the data to life with interactive, visually engaging dashboards, enabling easy tracking of monthly loan trends, funding levels, and risk profiles. These visualizations offered stakeholders a clear, data-driven perspective, supporting more effective decision-making around lending strategies and loan offerings.

Objectives:

  • Deliver a comprehensive review of loan portfolio performance, highlighting strengths and identifying areas for optimization.
  • Detect trends in loan applications, approval rates, and repayment behavior to support data-driven lending strategies.
  • Assess financial indicators such as Total Funded Amount, Average Interest Rate, and Loan Status to understand portfolio health.
  • Guide strategic decision-making for refining loan offerings to better align with market demands and borrower needs.
